----------------------------------------------------------------------------
INTERPRETATION NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Calibration lower/upper bounds:
   - Models scoring below all-known have LESS overconfidence than a naive oracle.
   - Models scoring near 'perfect' (1.0) have excellent uncertainty quantification.
   - The all-known/all-unknown gap narrows as n_hidden increases:
     easy gap: 0.500,  medium gap: 0.200,  hard gap: 0.000

2. Objective floor:
   No-op    = 0.4801  (rules fire but agent contributes nothing)
   Random   = 0.4719  (random actions, averaged over 20 seeds)
   Any model scoring at or near these values is not planning effectively.

3. n_hidden ablation:
   Objective typically falls with difficulty. If a model's easy-hard delta is
   near zero, it is either insensitive to uncertainty or saturation-floored.
